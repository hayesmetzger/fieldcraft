There's a moment every fly fisher knows: you're standing in a river that should be full of rising fish, your fly is drifting perfectly, and nothing is happening. You try your most reliable pattern. Then another. Then another. Still nothing.

Then you look at the water more carefully. There — a tiny insect, barely visible, drifting in the surface film. Not the mayfly you assumed. A midge. Something so small you almost missed it entirely.

The fish aren't being difficult. They're being precise. And you've been offering them something that has nothing to do with what they're actually eating.

Matching the hatch is the art of meeting the moment as it actually is, not as you assumed it would be.
