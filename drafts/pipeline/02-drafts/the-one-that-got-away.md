Every angler remembers the fish they lost more vividly than the ones they caught.

The weight of it on the line. The moment the hook pulled free. The way the water went still again as if nothing had happened. You replay it. You second-guess the knot, the hookset, the decision to press rather than let the fish run. You remember exactly where you were standing and what the light looked like.

You almost never replay the fish you landed with the same intensity.

There's something instructive in this. Failure makes a deeper impression than success — not because we're masochists, but because loss demands analysis in a way that success doesn't. When something works, we accept it and move on. When it doesn't, we can't stop asking why.
