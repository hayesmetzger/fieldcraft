I didn't start fishing alone because I preferred it. I started because the friends who fished were busy, or because the stretch of water I wanted to try was too narrow for two rods, or because the window opened unexpectedly on a Tuesday morning when no one else was free.

But somewhere along the way, fishing alone became the point.

There's a particular quality of attention that only comes when no one is watching. When you don't have to narrate your decisions or perform your expertise or fill the silence. When the only conversation is between you and the river, and you can take as long as you need to figure out what it's saying.

Most of us spend very little time in that state.
